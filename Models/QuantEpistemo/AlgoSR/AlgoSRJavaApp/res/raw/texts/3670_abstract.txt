Electoral maps are used both by a large public, as an information tool, and by scientists as a political and geographical analysis tool. The researcher has often to make comparisons between maps to get a better understanding of the political phénomenas that are going on. But making map comparisons is not that easy ! The purpose of this article is to draw some methodologic well-known possibilities in making maps comparisons, which can be applied to electoral geography to help to improve our knowledge of political phenomenas.
