Effectiveness of any —so-called— "natural risks" management policy depends greatly on the part assigned to representations, rumors and balance of power. Environment perception is a matter of negotiation and interpretation of facts that has to be part of risk management policies. It means that natural risks and natural disasters don't really exist. Hazard acceptability and its integration into urban and land planning is at stake here. Analyzing New-Orleans' recent disaster —hurricane Katrina— we try to give some light on these issues.
