This article aims at a reflection on the evolution of urban geography since 1970s. After the "quantitative revolution", urban geography in Japan has transformed its themes and research subjects. But for a long time it hardly touched upon the epistemological reflection concerning concepts and notions which were used in its empirical studies, especially space and place. However, after 1990, some geographers tended to reflect the implicit problems, linking field studies with an epistemological reflection.
